# DBT-Mind TikTok Scroll-Stopper Framework

A unified system for creating viral TikTok slideshow posts combining topic selection, hook text, and first image into one cohesive scroll-stopping unit.

---

## Part 1: Topic Selection

The topic is the foundation. A perfect hook and image cannot save a boring topic. Choose topics that hit emotional pain points your audience viscerally recognizes.

### Tier 1: Highest Virality Potential (Relationship/Attachment Topics)

These consistently get the highest engagement because they hit the deepest emotional pain points:

#### 1. Favorite Person (FP) Dynamics
- Losing your FP
- Splitting on your FP
- FP dependency/addiction
- What it feels like when your FP pulls away
- The difference between love and FP attachment
- When your FP doesn't text back

**Example hooks:**
- "The truth about your 'favorite person' that no one talks about"
- "Why losing your FP feels like actual death"
- "FP attachment isn't love. Here's what it actually is."

#### 2. Splitting
- What splitting looks like vs feels like
- Splitting on partners/friends
- The "enemy of the state" moment
- Black-and-white thinking in relationships
- When you split on someone you love

**Example hooks:**
- "What a BPD split actually feels like inside"
- "The moment they go from soulmate to enemy"
- "Why you can't see the gray area (and what to do about it)"

#### 3. Fear of Abandonment
- Pushing people away before they leave
- "Can't abandon me if I abandon you first"
- Relationship sabotage patterns
- Why calm feels like rejection
- Testing people to see if they'll stay
- Preemptive rejection

**Example hooks:**
- "Why you push away everyone who gets close"
- "The abandonment wound that controls all your relationships"
- "You're not scared of being alone. You're scared of being left."

#### 4. Relationship Cycles
- Idealization ‚Üí devaluation
- The breakup cycle (breaking up and going back)
- Why you keep choosing the same people
- Trauma bonding vs actual love
- When love feels like chaos

**Example hooks:**
- "The relationship cycle no one with BPD talks about"
- "Why you keep going back (it's not because you love them)"
- "That's not a connection. That's a trauma bond."

## Part 2: The Hook Text

Choose ONE primary hook type that matches your topic:

### 1. Forbidden Knowledge Hook
- "The BPD truth your therapist won't say out loud"
- "What mental health influencers won't tell you about BPD"
- "I got blocked for posting this"

**Best for topics:** Uncomfortable truths, therapy truths, reframes
**Best paired with:** Truth-teller expression, confident/mischievous energy

### 2. Specific Number Hook
- "5 BPD lies I believed for years"
- "3 things that changed after 6 months of DBT"
- "The 1 skill that stopped my spiraling"

**Best for topics:** DBT skills, signs/symptoms, recovery milestones
**Best paired with:** Calm authority, counting-on-fingers gesture

### 3. Pattern Interrupt Hook
- "You're not empathic. You're hypervigilant."
- "Stop romanticizing your survival mechanisms"
- "That's not love. That's withdrawal."

**Best for topics:** Reframes, relationship truths, identity topics
**Best paired with:** Intense eye contact, slight confrontational energy

### 4. Transformation Hook
- "6 months ago I was on the bathroom floor. Now I catch myself before I spiral."
- "How I went from splitting daily to actually trusting someone"

**Best for topics:** Recovery milestones, DBT success stories
**Best paired with:** Before/after image sequence OR hopeful-after-the-storm expression

## Part 3: The First Image (Scroll-Stopper)

The first image must STOP THE SCROLL. It carries the highest energy of the entire slideshow.

Must include **minimum 2** of these elements:

### A. Relatable Authenticity
- Average-looking person, not polished influencer
- Visible imperfection: dark circles, messy hair, no makeup, blotchy skin
- Real environment (see Setting Options below)
- iPhone mirror selfie or front camera selfie (not professional photography)

### B. Emotional Expression (match to hook type)

| Hook Type | Expression |
|-----------|------------|
| Forbidden Knowledge | Knowing smirk, raised eyebrow, "I know something" confidence |
| Specific Number | Calm authority, direct eye contact, teacher energy |
| Pattern Interrupt | Intense, slightly confrontational, truth-bomb energy |
| Transformation | Vulnerable but hopeful, soft strength, survivor energy |

### C. Expressive Gesture
- Hand raised with fingers pointing upward, tense grip like holding invisible ball = "let me tell you something"
- Finger pointing at camera = direct challenge
- Hand on chest = personal/emotional
- Counting fingers = specific number hook

### D. Curiosity/Engagement Element (choose at least one)

| Element | Why It Works |
|---------|--------------|
| Pet in background | Comment bait, softens mood, adds humor |
| Unusual background detail | Makes people look twice, sparks comments |
| Visible emotion (tears, red eyes) | Draws empathy, feels real |
| Contrasting elements | Messy room + confident pose = intriguing |

### E. Setting Options

Choose a setting that fits your hook and topic. The setting should feel real and relatable to your target audience.

| Setting | Vibe | Best For |
|---------|------|----------|
| Bathroom mirror (night) | Raw, 2am crisis energy, harsh lighting | Forbidden knowledge, pattern interrupt, FP/splitting topics |
| Bedroom mirror | Intimate, vulnerable, personal | Transformation, emotional topics, identity topics |
| Car selfie | Casual, "just had a realization" | Pattern interrupt, specific number, quick truths |
| Couch/living room | Relaxed but real, approachable | Specific number, softer topics, recovery content |
| Bed (lying down) | Late night, can't sleep, emotional | Deep emotional content, FP loss, fear of abandonment |
| Kitchen | Everyday life, relatable chaos | Lighter topics, daily struggles |
| Outdoors (golden hour) | Hope, transformation, new chapter | Transformation hooks, resolution, recovery milestones |

## Part 4: Slides 2-6 Image Strategy

### The Core Principle: Slide 1 = High Energy, Slides 2-6 = Calm Progression

**Why this works:**

1. **Authenticity** ‚Äî Real people don't dramatically change expression every 3 seconds. A subtle progression feels genuine.

2. **Text-image balance** ‚Äî If both image and text are intense, they compete. Strong text + calm-but-real image = text lands harder.

3. **Viewer fatigue** ‚Äî 6 slides of intense expressions becomes exhausting and performative.

4. **Let text do the work** ‚Äî Slides 2-6 deliver the content. The images support, they don't compete.

### Energy Distribution

| Slide | Image Energy | Purpose |
|-------|--------------|---------|
| 1 | HIGH - expressive, gesture, scroll-stopper | Stop the scroll, hook them |
| 2 | Calm - neutral, authentic | Deliver first truth, let text hit |
| 3 | Calm - subtle emotion okay | Deepen the point |
| 4 | Calm - slight shift toward thoughtful | The turn/realization |
| 5 | Calm - gentle warmth | Solution/app mention |
| 6 | Gentle hope - soft smile, resolution | Close with hope, CTA energy |

### Visual Consistency Rules

- **Same person** throughout all 6 slides
- **Same general environment** or logical progression of environments
- **Same pet** if using one (continuity matters)
- **Subtle lighting progression:** dark/harsh ‚Üí natural/warm (mirrors emotional journey)
- **Expression shifts subtly**, not dramatically between each slide

### Slide 2-6 Expression Guide

| Slide | Expression | What to Avoid |
|-------|------------|---------------|
| 2 | Neutral, direct, calm delivery | Don't match slide 1 intensity |
| 3 | Thoughtful, maybe slightly pained | Don't overact the emotion |
| 4 | Processing, reflective | Don't look confused |
| 5 | Soft recognition, gentle half-smile | Don't look like an ad |
| 6 | Genuine warmth, peaceful, grounded | Don't look fake-happy or over-transformed |

## Part 5: The Complete Formula

**Viral Post = Strong Topic + Matching Hook + Scroll-Stopper Image + Calm Progression**

### Step-by-Step Process:

1. **Choose Topic** (Part 1) ‚Üí Pick from Tier 1-3 based on virality goals
2. **Match Hook Type** (Part 2) ‚Üí Select hook framework that fits topic
3. **Design Slide 1 Image** (Part 3) ‚Üí Expression + gesture + setting + curiosity element
4. **Plan Slides 2-6** (Part 4) ‚Üí Calm progression, text does the work
5. **Write Slide Text** ‚Üí Deliver on the hook's promise
6. **Include App Mention** ‚Üí Slide 4, 5, or 6 (natural integration)

### Example Complete Build:

**Topic:** Splitting (Tier 1 - Relationship/Attachment)
**Hook Type:** Pattern Interrupt
**Hook Text:** "What a BPD split actually feels like inside"

**Slide 1 Image:**
- Expression: Intense, slightly confrontational, truth-bomb energy
- Gesture: Hand raised, tense "let me explain" pose
- Setting: Bathroom mirror, night, harsh lighting
- Curiosity Element: Cat on toilet

**Slides 2-6:**
- Calm expressions, same bathroom/bedroom
- Subtle lighting shift toward warmer
- Text carries emotional weight

**Slide Text Flow:**
1. "What a BPD split actually feels like inside"
2. "One second they're your whole world. The next they're the enemy."
3. "It's not a choice. Your brain literally can't hold both truths at once."

## Part 9: Common Mistakes to Avoid

### Topic Mistakes:
- ‚ùå Generic mental health advice that applies to everyone
- ‚ùå Topics that don't hit specific BPD/DBT pain points
- ‚ùå Educational content without personal experience angle
- ‚ùå Topics that are too niche (no broad relatability)
- ‚ùå Copying exact topics that are already saturated

### Hook Mistakes:
- ‚ùå Hook doesn't match topic category
- ‚ùå Too vague or generic ("Things about BPD")
- ‚ùå No curiosity gap or tension
- ‚ùå Trying to use multiple hook types at once

### Slide 1 Image Mistakes:
- ‚ùå Using a polished/attractive influencer look
- ‚ùå Professional photography lighting
- ‚ùå Neutral/passive expression that doesn't match hook energy
- ‚ùå No curiosity element (nothing to spark comments)
- ‚ùå Setting that doesn't match topic's emotional tone

### Slides 2-6 Mistakes:
- ‚ùå Matching slide 1's high energy on every slide
- ‚ùå Dramatic expression changes between each slide
- ‚ùå Different person or inconsistent appearance
- ‚ùå Forgetting the continuity element (pet disappears)
- ‚ùå Slide 6 looking like a fake transformation or ad

### Overall Mistakes:
- ‚ùå Images competing with text for attention
- ‚ùå Too polished = feels like an ad
- ‚ùå Too rough = uncomfortable to look at
- ‚ùå No visual progression = static and boring
- ‚ùå App mention feels forced or salesy
